About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Center Borelli under the surpervision of Jean-Michel Morel and Gabriele Facciolo. I am interested in image processing problems and inverse problems, with a particular enthusiasm for lens design and postprocessing. Recently I moved to video processing, specifically for real-time denoising and super-resolution tasks.

Before that, I was a PHD student at INRIA Paris, in the WILLOW project team specialized in computer vision, under the supervision of Jean Ponce and Jian Sun. I worked on hybrid methods for image deblurring, at the intersection of classical inverse problems and recent deep learning techniques. There, I also studied subsitute machine learning techniques to deep learning, in particular kernel methods.

Prior to research, I received an engineering degree in applied mathematics from Telecom Paris with majors in signal processing and distributed computation, as well as a M.Sc. degree in Applied mathematics (called MVA in France, standing for maths, vision and learning) from Ecole Normale Superieure de Paris-Saclay.

Beside my research activities, I spend time taking and editing photos, running and biking.
